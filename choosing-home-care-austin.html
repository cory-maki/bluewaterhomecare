How to Choose the Right Home Care Agency in Austin

📅 May 2025
✎ Jennifer Prescott, RN, MSN, CDP
⌚ 6 min read

Choosing a home care agency for an aging parent or loved one is one of the most important decisions a family in Austin will make — and the difference between agencies is far greater than most people realize.

The Austin area has seen significant growth in senior care providers over the past decade. Franchise operations, staffing agencies, and independent providers all compete for families' trust. But knowing what questions to ask — and what red flags to watch for — can help you find an agency that genuinely puts your loved one first.

Franchise vs. Independent: Why Ownership Structure Matters

Many home care agencies operating in Austin are franchises of national brands. That structure has real implications for your family. Franchise agencies pay royalty fees to their parent company — fees that come out of the budget that could otherwise go toward caregiver wages, training, and retention. Lower caregiver pay typically means higher turnover, which directly affects the continuity of care your loved one receives.

Independent, locally owned agencies like Blue Water Homecare & Hospice operate without that overhead. Every dollar goes toward delivering better care. Blue Water's founder Jennifer Prescott built the agency without a franchise model specifically because she wanted full control over hiring standards, ongoing education, and the culture of care. That independence allows Blue Water to maintain a higher percentage of certified caregivers than other local homecare organizations in Central Texas.

"Not all home care is created equal. The most important thing a family can do is ask hard questions — about turnover, certification rates, and what oversight actually looks like day to day." — Jennifer Prescott, RN, MSN, CDP

Questions Every Family Should Ask Before Choosing

When evaluating home care agencies in Austin, start with these questions. A trustworthy agency will answer them without hesitation. What is your caregiver turnover rate? How do you verify caregiver certifications and background checks? What happens if a scheduled caregiver doesn't show up? Do you offer real-time updates or a family portal? Are your supervisors registered nurses?

At Blue Water, every answer is documented and transparent. The agency's care portal gives families real-time visibility into what's happening in the home — not just a weekly phone call from a coordinator. The team is led by Registered Nursing professionals, and ongoing caregiver education happens weekly or monthly across every discipline. You can learn more about the Blue Water team's credentials and history on the about page.

Specialized Care vs. General Home Assistance

If your loved one is living with dementia, Parkinson's disease, or a serious terminal illness, general home assistance may not be enough. Specialized care requires caregivers who have been trained in the specific behavioral, physical, and cognitive patterns of those conditions — and a clinical leadership team that can adapt the care plan as the disease progresses.

Blue Water Homecare & Hospice offers structured specialty programs in dementia care and Parkinson's support that go well beyond basic personal care. These aren't add-on services — they're purpose-built programs developed by Jennifer Prescott and the Blue Water clinical team. If your family is navigating a specific diagnosis, read our guide on dementia care at home in Central Texas for more detail on what specialized support looks like in practice.

The Hospice Question: Planning Ahead

Families often don't think about hospice until a crisis forces the conversation. But having a relationship with a trusted provider before that moment arrives makes an enormous difference. Blue Water's boutique hospice model — with a dedicated RN Case Manager, 24/7 nursing coverage, and a multidisciplinary team including chaplains and social workers — is designed to ease that transition and keep your loved one comfortable at home.

Understanding what hospice involves in advance allows families to make decisions thoughtfully, not reactively. Our article on what hospice care at home means for Austin families walks through the timeline, the team structure, and the emotional preparation families need.

Conclusion

The best home care agency for your family in Austin is one that treats your loved one the way they would treat their own family member. Blue Water Homecare & Hospice was built on exactly that standard.
